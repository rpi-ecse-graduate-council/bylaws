\Article{I}{Name}
The name of this organization shall be the \textbf{\orgname} (hereafter, the \textbf{\orgshort}).

\Article{II}{Purpose}
The \orgshort{} exists to improve the graduate student experience within the Department of Electrical, Computer, and Systems Engineering (ECSE) by (i) building community among graduate, (ii) advocating for graduate student needs to departmental and Institute leadership, (iii) organizing social, professional, and wellness programming, and (iv) supporting initiatives that enhance learning, research, and everyday life for \depname{} graduate students.

\Article{III}{Membership}

\Sec{1. Equal Opportunity and Hazing}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item It is the policy of the \orgshort{} to provide, to the maximum extent feasible, equal opportunity to all qualified individuals without regard to race, ethnicity, color, religion, national origin, gender, sexual orientation, or disability for all aspects of the \orgshort{}'s activities.
    \item Hazing, reckless or intentional endangerment to health, or forced consumption of alcohol or other drugs for the purpose of initiation into or continuing membership of the \orgshort{} is strictly prohibited.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{2. Definition of Membership}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item \textbf{Regular Members} are registered Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (RPI) students who are enrolled in graduate programs offered by the \depname{} Department subject to a maximum of eight (8).
    \item \textbf{Associate Members} (non-voting) may include but not limited to \depname{} faculty/staff, \depname{} alumni, and non-\depname{} RPI students interested in supporting \orgshort{} activities.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{3. Obtaining Membership}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item To obtain Regular Membership, an eligible person must be nominated by any active graduate student in the \depname{} department and must be voted in during regular annual elections.
    \item Associate Membership may be granted through approval by the \orgshort{} upon receipt of written request indicating such intent.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{4. Continued Membership}
Every regular member of \orgshort{} voted in during regular annual elections shall continue to hold office for 1 year or until the next elections, as the case may be.

\Sec{5. Voting Rights}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item Only Regular Members in good standing shall have voting rights; each such member has one vote.
    \item A member loses voting rights upon loss of Regular Member status or for cause as determined through the disciplinary process in Section~7.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{6. Membership Information}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item The \orgshort{} shall collect, record and maintain an updated roster of: (i) preferred name, (ii) RIN (if applicable), (iii) RCS ID (if applicable), (iv) email address of all regularly registered grad students in the \depname{} department.
    \item All information other than names and membership level is confidential and released only to the department staff upon specific request received in writing to \orgshort{}.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{7. Removal of Membership}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item A member may be removed for failure to comply with these bylaws or \orgshort{} policy, violation of the Student Code of Conduct, or violation of RPI Handbook of Student Rights \& Responsibilities.

    \item \textbf{Process:} (i) A written complaint is submitted to the \orgshort{} voting membership; (ii) removal requires a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the \orgshort{} voting membership; (iv) notice of decision and appeal options is provided in writing within five (5) business days.
\end{enumerate}

\Article{IV}{Officers}

\Sec{1. Officers and Duties}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item The \orgshort{} shall include at minimum a \textbf{President}, a \textbf{Secretary} and two \textbf{Officers}. 
    \item \textbf{President:} It shall be the President's duty to lead the \orgshort{}, set agendas, represent graduate students to \depname{} leadership and Institute bodies, and ensure alignment with the \orgshort{} purpose. The \orgshort{} President may also appoint the following roles to reflect \orgshort{} activities and needs
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Vice President:} Supports the President; oversees committees; manages internal operations and succession planning.
    \item \textbf{Events Coordinator(s):} Plans social/professional events that build cross-group interactions (e.g., mixers, seminars); coordinates logistics and evaluations.
    \item \textbf{Publicity \& Communications Coordinator (s):} Manages outreach (email, flyers, social); maintains web/CMS presence.
\end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}
\Sec{2. Eligibility for Office}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item Candidates must be Regular Members.
    \item If an officer cannot complete the term, they shall inform the \orgshort{} before being eligible to hold office in the future.
    \item No person may hold more than one elected office concurrently.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{3. Elections}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item Elections shall be held at least once per academic year, typically in early November for terms beginning in the subsequent week of elections.
    \item \textbf{Process:} (i) Graduate Program Assistant announces the election timeline at least two (2) weeks in advance; (ii) nominations open for one (1) week and may be self-nominations; (iii) candidates submit a short statement; (iv) voting occurs at a General Meeting or via secured online ballot open for 48 hours; (v) simple majority elects; (vi) ties are resolved by a runoff between tied candidates.
    \item Any member who suspects unfair conduct shall notify the Graduate Program Assistant for further scrutiny.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{4. Removal from Office}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item An officer may be removed for failure to perform duties, failure to comply with these bylaws or \orgshort{} policy, violation of the Student Code of Conduct, or violation of \depname{} Department policies.
    \item \textbf{Process:} (i) Written petition by at least five (5) Regular Members or a majority of the \orgshort{}; (ii) discussion at a special meeting with at least five (5) days' notice; (iii) removal requires two-thirds (2/3) vote of Regular Members present and voting, provided quorum is met.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{5. Succession}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item Vacancies shall be filled as soon as reasonably achievable.
    \item \textbf{Process:} The Vice President serves as Acting President if the Presidency is vacant; other vacancies are filled by special election using Article IV, Section 3 procedures within three (3) weeks.
\end{enumerate}

\Article{V}{Meetings}

\Sec{1. General Meetings}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item The \orgshort{} shall hold a minimum of two (2) General Meetings each Fall and Spring semester.
    \item \textbf{Procedure \& Quorum:} Meetings follow an agenda set by the President and distributed at least 24 hours in advance. Quorum for business is fifty percent (50\%) of Regular Members or three (3) Regular Members, whichever is fewer. Proxy or electronic participation may count toward quorum if allowed by the President. Actions pass by simple majority unless otherwise specified.
\end{enumerate}

\Article{VI}{Policies, Standing Rules, and By-Laws}

\Sec{1. Availability}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item All policies, standing rules, and by-laws shall be available to any Activity-Fee-paying student or interested member upon request and shall be published wherever these bylaws are published. These bylaws must be published on the \orgshort{} website, and on file with the \depname{} department administration.
\end{enumerate}

\Article{VII}{Ratification and Amendments}

\Sec{1. Supremacy Clause}
If any part of these bylaws violates or contradicts the policies of Rensselaer, the Rensselaer Union, or applicable laws, the contradictory parts are invalid.

\Sec{2. Ratification}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item These bylaws shall be effective immediately upon ratification by a two-thirds (2/3) majority of members with voting rights and subsequent approval of the \depname{} department administration.
    \item Amendments require a two-thirds (2/3) vote of members with voting rights present at the amendment meeting and subsequent approval by the \depname{} department administration.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{3. Abrogation}
Upon adoption, these bylaws abrogates previous governing documents relating to the \orgshort{} without disrupting officers currently serving terms validly elected under a prior set of bylaws.
