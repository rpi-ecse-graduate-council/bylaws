\Article{I}{Name}
The name of this organization shall be the \textbf{\orgname} (hereafter, the \textbf{\orgshort}).

\Article{II}{Purpose}
The \orgshort{} exists to improve the graduate student experience within the Department of Electrical, Computer, and Systems Engineering (ECSE) by (i) building community among graduate students across research groups, (ii) advocating for graduate student needs to departmental and Institute leadership, (iii) organizing social, professional, and wellness programming, and (iv) supporting initiatives that enhance learning, research, and everyday life for ECSE graduate students.

\Article{III}{Membership}

\Sec{1. Equal Opportunity and Hazing}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item It is the policy of the \orgshort{} to provide, to the maximum extent feasible, equal opportunity to all qualified individuals without regard to race, ethnicity, color, religion, national origin, gender, sexual orientation, or disability for all aspects of the \orgshort{}'s activities.
    \item Hazing, reckless or intentional endangerment to health, or forced consumption of alcohol or other drugs for the purpose of initiation into or continuing membership of the \orgshort{} is strictly prohibited.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{2. Definition of Membership}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item \textbf{Regular Members} are registered Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (RPI) students who are enrolled in graduate programs offered by the ECSE Department.
    \item \textbf{Associate Members} (non-voting) may include ECSE faculty/staff, ECSE alumni, and non-ECSE RPI students interested in supporting \orgshort{} activities.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{3. Obtaining Membership}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item To obtain Regular Membership, an eligible person must attend xx meeting or event, complete the membership sign-up, and (if applicable) pay dues established by the \orgshort{}.
    \item Associate Membership is granted upon sign-up and approval by the ECSE GC Executive Board.
    \item The ECSC GC Executive Board may verify membership eligibility through the Office of the Registrar or Bursar.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{4. Continued Membership}
A Regular Member remains in good standing by (i) being an Activity-Fee-paying student, (ii) adhering to this Constitution and \orgshort{} policies, and (iii) participating in at least xx \orgshort{} meeting or event per semester.

\Sec{5. Voting Rights}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item Only Regular Members in good standing shall have voting rights; each such member has one vote.
    \item A member loses voting rights upon loss of Regular Member status or for cause as determined through the disciplinary process in Section~7.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{6. Membership Information}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item The \orgshort{} shall collect: (i) preferred name, (ii) RIN (if applicable), (iii) RCS ID (if applicable), (iv) email address, (v) dues payment record (if any), (vi) other information required by the Rensselaer Union Executive Board.
    \item All information other than names and membership level is confidential and released only to the Rensselaer Union Executive Board and Union administration staff as required.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{7. Removal of Membership}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item A member may be removed for failure to comply with this Constitution or \orgshort{} policy, violation of the Student Code of Conduct, or violation of RPI Handbook of Student Rights \& Responsibilities.

    \item \textbf{Process:} (i) A written complaint is submitted to the Secretary; (ii) the Executive Board reviews and may invite statements; (iii) removal requires a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Executive Board; (iv) notice of decision and appeal options is provided in writing within five (5) business days.
\end{enumerate}

\Article{IV}{Officers}

\Sec{1. Officers and Duties}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item The \orgshort{} shall include at minimum a \textbf{President}, a \textbf{Secretary} and two \textbf{Student Representatives}. 
    \item \textbf{President:} It shall be the President's duty to lead the \orgshort{}, set agendas, represent graduate students to ECSE leadership and Institute bodies, and ensure alignment with the \orgshort{} purpose. The \orgshort{} President may also appoint the following roles to reflect ECSE GC activities and needs
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Vice President:} Supports the President; oversees committees; manages internal operations and succession planning.
    \item \textbf{Events Coordinator(s):} Plans social/professional events that build cross-group interactions (e.g., mixers, seminars); coordinates logistics and evaluations.
    \item \textbf{Publicity \& Communications Coordinator (s):} Manages outreach (email, flyers, social); maintains web/CMS presence.
    \item \textbf{Student Representatives:} Interfaces with ECSE Graduate Student Services and departmental leadership to surface student concerns and opportunities.
\end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}
\Sec{2. Eligibility for Office}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item Candidates must be Regular Members.
    \item If an officer cannot complete the term, they shall inform the \orgshort{} before being eligible to hold office in the future.
    \item No person may hold more than one elected office concurrently.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{3. Elections}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item Elections shall be held at least once per academic year, typically in March--April for terms beginning in May.
    \item \textbf{Process:} (i) Secretary announces the election timeline at least two (2) weeks in advance; (ii) nominations open for one (1) week and may be self-nominations; (iii) candidates submit a short statement; (iv) voting occurs at a General Meeting or via secured online ballot open for 48 hours; (v) simple majority elects; (vi) ties are resolved by a runoff between tied candidates.
    \item Any member who suspects unfair conduct shall notify the faculty advisor.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{4. Removal from Office}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item An officer may be removed for failure to perform duties, failure to comply with this Constitution or \orgshort{} policy, violation of the Student Code of Conduct, or violation of ECSE Department policies.
    \item \textbf{Process:} (i) Written petition by at least five (5) Regular Members or a majority of the Executive Board; (ii) discussion at a special meeting with at least five (5) days' notice; (iii) removal requires two-thirds (2/3) vote of Regular Members present, provided quorum is met.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{5. Succession}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item Vacancies shall be filled as soon as reasonably achievable.
    \item \textbf{Process:} The Vice President serves as Acting President if the Presidency is vacant; other vacancies are filled by special election using Article IV, Section 3 procedures within three (3) weeks.
\end{enumerate}

\Article{V}{Meetings}

\Sec{1. General Meetings}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item The \orgshort{} shall hold a minimum of two (2) General Meetings each Fall and Spring semester.
    \item \textbf{Procedure \& Quorum:} Meetings follow an agenda set by the President and distributed at least 24 hours in advance. Quorum for business is ten percent (10\%) of Regular Members or fifteen (15) Regular Members, whichever is fewer. Proxy or electronic participation may count toward quorum if allowed in adopted by-laws. Actions pass by simple majority unless otherwise specified.
\end{enumerate}

\Article{VI}{Finance (Required for Recognized Clubs)}

\Sec{1. Budget}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item The Treasurer solicits budget proposals from members and compiles a draft budget.
    \item The draft budget is presented to the membership and approved by majority vote.
    \item The Treasurer works with the \orgshort{}'s Rensselaer Union Executive Board representative to finalize the budget for submission and approval.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{2. Financial Procedures}
The \orgshort{} shall follow all Rensselaer Union financial policies and procedures, including handling of dues, donations, purchases, reimbursements, and fundraising.

\Article{VII}{Policies, Standing Rules, and By-Laws}

\Sec{1. Availability}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item All policies, standing rules, and by-laws shall be available to any Activity-Fee-paying student or interested member upon request and shall be published wherever this Constitution is published. This Constitution must be published on CMS, the \orgshort{} website, and on file with the Union.
    \item A copy of the by-laws must be filed with the Rensselaer Union Administration Office within five (5) business days following approval.
\end{enumerate}

\Article{VIII}{Ratification and Amendments}

\Sec{1. Supremacy Clause}
If any part of this Constitution violates or contradicts the policies of Rensselaer, the Rensselaer Union, or applicable laws, the contradictory parts are invalid.

\Sec{2. Ratification}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item This Constitution shall be effective immediately upon ratification by a two-thirds (2/3) majority of members with voting rights and subsequent approval of the Rensselaer Union Executive Board or properly delegated authority.
    \item Amendments require a two-thirds (2/3) vote of members with voting rights present at the amendment meeting and subsequent approval by the Rensselaer Union Executive Board or properly delegated authority.
\end{enumerate}

\Sec{3. Abrogation}
Upon adoption, this Constitution abrogates previous governing documents relating to the \orgshort{} without disrupting officers currently serving terms validly elected under a prior constitution.
